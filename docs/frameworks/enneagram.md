# Enneagram Framework Reference

## Overview

The Enneagram identifies nine personality types organized into three centers (gut, heart, head). Each type has a core motivation, a core fear, and a growth direction. Unlike Big Five, Enneagram is categorical, not dimensional.

## Three Centers

### Gut Center (Body/Instinctive) - Types 8, 9, 1
**Core theme:** Anger and autonomy
**Selection paragraph:** "You lead with instinct. Your first response to the world is physical, a gut reaction. You know what's right before you can explain why, and your challenge is what to do with that certainty."

### Heart Center (Feeling/Image) - Types 2, 3, 4
**Core theme:** Shame and identity
**Selection paragraph:** "You lead with feeling. Your first response to the world is emotional. You're tuned to how others see you and how you see yourself, and your challenge is finding who you are beneath those reflections."

### Head Center (Thinking/Fear) - Types 5, 6, 7
**Core theme:** Fear and security
**Selection paragraph:** "You lead with thinking. Your first response to the world is to analyze, question, or plan. You need to understand before you act, and your challenge is trusting that you know enough to move forward."

## Nine Types

### Type 1 - The Reformer (Gut)
- **Core motivation:** To be good, to have integrity, to be right
- **Core fear:** Being corrupt, evil, or defective
- **Selection paragraph:** "You hold yourself to a standard most people don't even see. There's a voice inside that always knows how things should be, and your deepest drive is to close the gap between what is and what ought to be."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You notice what needs fixing in any situation" / "You notice what's working in any situation"
  - "You hold yourself to higher standards than others hold you" / "You're generally satisfied with your own efforts"

### Type 9 - The Peacemaker (Gut)
- **Core motivation:** To have inner stability and peace of mind
- **Core fear:** Loss, fragmentation, separation
- **Selection paragraph:** "You see every side of every situation, which is both your gift and your burden. Your deepest drive is to maintain inner peace and connection, even when it means setting aside your own voice to keep things whole."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You often go along with what others want" / "You usually push for what you want"
  - "Conflict drains you more than almost anything" / "Conflict energizes you to find a resolution"

### Type 8 - The Challenger (Gut)
- **Core motivation:** To be self-reliant, to protect themselves and others
- **Core fear:** Being harmed or controlled by others
- **Selection paragraph:** "You move through the world with force and directness. Your deepest drive is to be strong enough that no one can control you, and to use that strength to protect what matters."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You'd rather be respected than liked" / "You'd rather be liked than respected"
  - "You take charge in uncertain situations" / "You wait to see how things unfold"

### Type 2 - The Helper (Heart)
- **Core motivation:** To feel loved, to be needed
- **Core fear:** Being unwanted or unworthy of love
- **Selection paragraph:** "You feel other people's needs almost before they do. Your deepest drive is to be loved, and you've learned that the surest path to love is to become indispensable to the people around you."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You often know what someone needs before they ask" / "You wait for people to tell you what they need"
  - "Helping others energizes you" / "Helping others drains you if it's too frequent"

### Type 3 - The Achiever (Heart)
- **Core motivation:** To feel valuable and worthwhile
- **Core fear:** Being worthless or without inherent value
- **Selection paragraph:** "You adapt and excel in almost any environment you enter. Your deepest drive is to be valued for what you achieve, and you have an almost unconscious ability to become whatever a situation requires."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You measure yourself by what you've accomplished" / "You measure yourself by who you are regardless of accomplishments"
  - "You naturally adapt to what each situation requires" / "You stay the same regardless of the situation"

### Type 4 - The Individualist (Heart)
- **Core motivation:** To find themselves and their significance
- **Core fear:** Having no identity or personal significance
- **Selection paragraph:** "You feel things at a depth that most people don't access. Your deepest drive is to understand who you truly are and to express that identity, especially the parts that feel different from everyone else."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You often feel fundamentally different from others" / "You generally feel like you fit in"
  - "Melancholy has a beauty to it that you understand" / "You avoid sadness whenever possible"

### Type 5 - The Investigator (Head)
- **Core motivation:** To be capable and competent
- **Core fear:** Being useless, helpless, or incapable
- **Selection paragraph:** "You need to understand the world before you engage with it. Your deepest drive is to build enough knowledge and resources that you'll never be caught unprepared or overwhelmed by demands you can't meet."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You observe before you participate" / "You participate to figure things out"
  - "You need time alone to process and recharge" / "Processing happens naturally as you go about your day"

### Type 6 - The Loyalist (Head)
- **Core motivation:** To have security and support
- **Core fear:** Being without support or guidance
- **Selection paragraph:** "You see the risks that others miss. Your deepest drive is to find something trustworthy, whether a person, a system, or a belief, and your mind naturally scans for what could go wrong so you can prepare."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You tend to question authority and systems" / "You tend to trust established structures"
  - "You prepare for worst-case scenarios" / "You trust things will work out"

### Type 7 - The Enthusiast (Head)
- **Core motivation:** To be satisfied and content
- **Core fear:** Being deprived or trapped in pain
- **Selection paragraph:** "You are drawn to possibility. Your deepest drive is to stay free, stimulated, and open to experience, and you instinctively move toward joy and away from anything that feels limiting or painful."
- **Confirmation swipe examples:**
  - "You'd rather have too many options than too few" / "You prefer to go deep on one thing at a time"
  - "You naturally reframe negatives into positives" / "You sit with difficult feelings when they come"

## Assessment Flow in Selfscape

1. Show three center cards (gut/heart/head). User selects one.
2. Show three type cards within chosen center. User selects one.
3. Show 3-4 confirmation swipe pairs designed to discriminate within the center.
4. Score: if confirmation swipes agree with type selection, high confidence. If they point to a different type within the same center, note both as primary and possible alternative.
